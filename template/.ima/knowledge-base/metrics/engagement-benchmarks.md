# Engagement Rate Benchmarks

## How to Calculate Engagement Rate

### Standard Formula (Most Common)
```
ER = (Likes + Comments + Saves) / Follower Count × 100
```

### Reach-Based ER (More Accurate)
```
ER by Reach = (Likes + Comments + Saves) / Reach × 100
```

### Per-Post ER (For Comparison)
```
Average ER = Sum of ER per post (last 10-12 posts) / Number of posts
```

**Important:** Always exclude obvious outlier posts (viral spikes) when calculating average ER.

## How to Interpret ER

| Rating | Instagram ER | TikTok ER | What It Means |
|--------|:---:|:---:|---------------|
| ⭐ Excellent | >5% | >10% | Highly engaged audience, strong influence |
| 🟢 Good | 3-5% | 6-10% | Above average, quality engagement |
| 🟡 Average | 1.5-3% | 4-6% | Normal for the tier, acceptable |
| 🟠 Below Average | 0.5-1.5% | 2-4% | May indicate disengaged audience |
| 🔴 Poor | <0.5% | <2% | Likely fake followers or dead audience |

## Key Principles

1. **Smaller accounts = higher ER** — This is normal, not a sign of superiority
2. **Compare within tiers** — Don't compare a nano's ER to a macro's ER
3. **Niche matters** — Some niches naturally have higher ER than others
4. **Quality > quantity** — 100 genuine comments > 1000 emoji comments
5. **Reels/video > static** — Video formats consistently get higher ER
6. **Sponsored vs organic** — Expect 10-30% lower ER on sponsored content
7. **Trend adjustment** — These benchmarks shift over time; verify with current data
