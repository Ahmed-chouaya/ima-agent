# Influencer Marketing Best Practices

## Strategic Best Practices

### 1. Prioritize Authenticity Over Reach
- Choose influencers who genuinely align with your brand values
- Let creators use their own voice — audiences can spot inauthentic content
- Long-term partnerships build more trust than one-off posts
- Micro and nano influencers often deliver better ROI than mega influencers

### 2. Set Clear Objectives First
- Define SMART goals before selecting influencers or platforms
- Align influencer selection with specific campaign objectives
- Match metrics to objectives (awareness → reach; conversion → sales)
- Don't try to achieve everything in one campaign

### 3. Give Creative Freedom
- Provide guidelines, not scripts
- Trust creators to know their audience
- Over-controlling content leads to inauthentic, poor-performing posts
- Review and approve, but don't micromanage
- The best sponsored content feels organic

### 4. Build Long-Term Relationships
- Ambassador programs outperform one-off campaigns
- Consistent presence builds audience familiarity and trust
- Negotiate better rates with long-term commitments
- Re-engage top performers from previous campaigns first
- Treat influencers as partners, not vendors

### 5. Diversify Your Approach
- Mix influencer tiers (nano + micro + mid) for balanced reach + engagement
- Use multiple platforms to reach different audience segments
- Vary content types (video, photos, stories, long-form)
- Test different messaging angles and creative approaches

## Content Best Practices

### Do's ✅
- Let the product appear naturally in the creator's lifestyle
- Brief influencers with key messages, not word-for-word scripts
- Encourage storytelling around the product, not just features
- Include clear calls to action (but make them feel natural)
- Provide high-quality product and brand assets if needed
- Allow sufficient time for content creation (minimum 2 weeks)

### Don'ts ❌
- Don't ask influencers to read from a script
- Don't require exact word-for-word captions
- Don't ask for content that doesn't fit their aesthetic
- Don't expect immediate results from a single post
- Don't compare results across different tiers without context
- Don't ignore disclosure requirements

## Measurement Best Practices

### Track What Matters
- Focus on 2-3 KPIs per campaign, not everything
- Use UTM parameters for all trackable links
- Create unique promo codes per influencer for attribution
- Set up conversion tracking before campaign launch
- Compare against benchmarks, not arbitrary targets

### Report Honestly
- Include both successes and underperformance
- Benchmark against industry standards, not aspirational targets
- Account for brand lift and earned media value, not just direct conversions
- Track long-term impact (30/60/90 day windows), not just day-of

## Relationship Best Practices

### Communication
- Respond to influencer inquiries within 24 hours
- Be transparent about budget and expectations
- Provide constructive feedback, never harsh criticism
- Share results and celebrate successes together

### Compensation
- Pay fair market rates (reference `metrics/pricing-guides.md`)
- Pay on time (net-30 maximum, sooner is better)
- Value their work — don't ask for free content from established creators
- Consider performance bonuses for exceptional results

### Post-Campaign
- Thank influencers within 48 hours of content going live
- Share performance results with them
- Engage with their content beyond campaign periods
- Ask for feedback to improve future collaborations
- Keep them on your radar for future campaigns

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Choosing by follower count only | Ignores engagement quality | Use IMA scoring framework |
| Sending mass generic outreach | Low response rate, bad impression | Personalize with `/outreach` |
| No written agreement | Disputes, unclear expectations | Always have a contract |
| Ignoring FTC disclosure | Legal liability for brand and creator | See `compliance/ftc-guidelines.md` |
| One-off campaigns only | No brand recognition buildup | Build long-term partnerships |
| Over-controlling content | Inauthentic, poor performance | Provide guidelines, not scripts |
| Not tracking results | Can't prove ROI, can't optimize | Set up tracking before launch |
| Paying late | Damages relationship, reputation | Pay within agreed terms |
