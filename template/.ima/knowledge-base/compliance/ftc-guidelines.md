# FTC Disclosure & Legal Compliance Guide

## Core Rule
**If there is a material connection between a brand and an influencer, it must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously.** This includes paid partnerships, free products, affiliate relationships, and any other form of compensation.

---

## What Requires Disclosure

| Situation | Disclosure Required? |
|-----------|:-------------------:|
| Paid cash for content creation | ✅ Yes |
| Free product received (even without requirement to post) | ✅ Yes |
| Affiliate link/commission | ✅ Yes |
| Brand ambassador program | ✅ Yes |
| Employee of the brand | ✅ Yes |
| Stock ownership in the brand | ✅ Yes |
| Family relationship with brand owner | ✅ Yes |
| Gifted product they genuinely love | ✅ Yes |
| Purchased product with own money, no relationship | ❌ No |

## Disclosure Requirements by Platform

### Instagram
- Use the **Paid Partnership** tag (branded content tool)
- Include `#ad` or `#sponsored` in the **first line** of the caption
- **Not acceptable:** Burying `#ad` in a cluster of 20+ hashtags
- **Stories:** Disclosure must be visible as text overlay
- **Reels:** Include disclosure in text overlay AND caption

### TikTok
- Enable the **Branded Content** toggle
- Include `#ad` or `#sponsored` visibly in caption
- **Best practice:** Also verbally mention it's sponsored in the video
- Disclosure must be visible without expanding the caption

### YouTube
- Enable **"Includes Paid Promotion"** in YouTube Studio settings
- **Verbal disclosure** in the first 30 seconds of the video
- Written disclosure in video description (first 2 lines visible)
- For integrations: Disclose before the sponsored segment begins

### X (Twitter)
- Include `#ad` or `#sponsored` in the tweet
- For threads: Disclose in the first tweet
- Don't rely on `#partner` alone — use clear language

### LinkedIn
- Include `#ad` or `#sponsored` at the beginning of the post
- No built-in branded content tool — must be manual

## Disclosure Language Guide

### ✅ Acceptable Disclosures
- `#ad`
- `#sponsored`
- `Paid partnership with [Brand]`
- `[Brand] gifted me this product`
- `This video is sponsored by [Brand]`
- `Ad | [Brand]`

### ❌ Not Acceptable
- `#sp` (too abbreviated)
- `#partner` alone (ambiguous)
- `#collab` alone (ambiguous)
- `Thanks to [Brand]` without `#ad` (sounds organic)
- Disclosure only in alt text or image descriptions
- Disclosure buried at the end of a long caption
- `#ambassador` without clear sponsor language

## International Considerations

| Region | Key Requirements |
|--------|-----------------|
| **USA** | FTC Act Section 5 — "clear and conspicuous" disclosure |
| **UK** | ASA/CAP Code — "Ad" or "Advertisement" label required |
| **EU** | Unfair Commercial Practices Directive — varies by country |
| **UAE** | National Media Council regulations — disclosure required |
| **Australia** | AANA Code — clearly identifiable as advertising |
| **Canada** | Competition Act — no materially misleading representations |

## Brand Responsibilities

As a brand working with influencers, you are required to:
1. **Educate influencers** about disclosure requirements
2. **Include disclosure clauses** in all contracts
3. **Monitor content** to ensure disclosures are present
4. **Enforce compliance** — if an influencer doesn't disclose, you're also liable
5. **Document everything** — keep records of compensation and agreements

## Contract Clause Template
Include in every influencer agreement:
```
Influencer agrees to clearly and conspicuously disclose the material 
connection between Influencer and Brand in all content created under 
this agreement, in compliance with FTC guidelines and applicable local 
laws. Disclosure must appear as "#ad" or "Paid partnership with [Brand]" 
in a prominent position visible without expanding captions or descriptions.
```
